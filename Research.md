<p style="font-size: 25px; text-align: center;  font-weight: bold"> Undergraduate Research </p>
<p style="font-size: 20px; text-align: center"> Fall 2024 - Spring 2025</p>
During the 2024-2025 academic year I worked with Assistant Professor Samuel Harris and fellow undergraduate Taylor Smith on an Undergraduate Research project on Quantum Graph Theory. We studied properties of graphs that are "quantum-colored", quantum-colored graphs' vertices are labeled with matrix valued solutions, as opposed to "coloring" each vertex with a single number like in the classical way. We discovered the smallest graph that can be quantum 3-colored but not classically 3-colored by taking a known graph with these properties and removing superfluous vertices to get a graph with 110 vertices. This was done through SageMath by creating code scripts  that removed vertices and then checked that graphs still satisfy the conditions to be considered quantum 3-colorable. A paper on this research is being worked on. <br>
This research culminated in an oral and poster presentation at the 2025 NAU Undergraduate Symposium. This research was also presented at the NAU Mathematics and Statistics Departments' Friday Afternoon Mathematics Undergraduate Symposium (FAMUS) and at the 2025 Southwestern Undergraduate Mathematics Research Conference (SUnMaRC).
